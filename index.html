Training the Kenyan
Way.

A while back, I was training a young man who had never ran before. His goal was to complete a half marathon within 9 months. The first few weeks were tough on him, he had to push his body hard every day just to get there. On the day of the race, he ended up being the 100th person to cross the finish line in 3 hours, he came up to me and said " I thought you were supposed to make me better, I thought you were supossed to help me win. I lost!" Later that day, I sat him down and we started looking at the jouney that he had had to get to that moment, all the mornings he woke up and he wanted to quit, all the days he wanted to give up bacause it was tough but he pushed through. He had just ran his first marathon ever, that was a victory enough, 9 months ago he could not have ran a marathon and finished, 'today he was a winner' .

He kept training and when he went for his next marathon, he finished within 2 hrs 42 munites. The next time he rans in a marathon, he will not be looking at what position he was, but how better he was this time than he was last time. That is what i do, I train people to be their best selves, so they can achieve their dreams.
